Two technologies :: deep & niche
01

High precision is the key that unlocks semiclassical physics

The physics that matters most lives between quantum and classical, and it cannot be computed correctly at standard 64-bit precision — double precision quietly degrades. Computing beyond 64 bits is the unlock, not a feature. We are building the whole stack around it.

02

High precision demands software–hardware co-design

It does not run well on generic chips and frameworks. So Attryx is built from its own language and kernel-level operations — precision controlled where it lives, at the kernel — and that same software is the instrument that informs the silicon.

Moat ::

Two-sided and hard to copy — a high-precision software stack down to the kernel, on a credible path to co-designed hardware.

A hundreds-of-billions market :: one shared bottleneck

Semiclassical physics is the hidden computation layer underneath enormous, separate markets that all bottleneck on the same problem:

Sub-nanometer semiconductors Quantum sensing — e.g. gyroscopes Spintronic memory (MRAM) Advanced materials & coatings Molecular & protein dynamics

They look unrelated, but share one foundational need: high-precision semiclassical computation. Own that layer and you sit underneath all of them at once.

Why now ::

AI has made generating candidates — molecules, materials, device designs — cheap, so the constraint shifts to the physics layer that verifies whether a candidate is real, and that layer matters more as AI accelerates.
